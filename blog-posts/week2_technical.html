Hanging the Mona Lisa

July 6, 2014

Let's talk about margins, borders and padding within the context of hanging a painting on a wall. Turns out I just got a great deal on a classic work of art, so the timing is perfect.

1 - First we start off with examining what we have - a wall and a painting.

2 - If I tell my webpage to put my Mona Lisa on the wall, we get the following. It just plunks her down in the default position of the top left corner since I haven't specified otherwise.

3 - In order to position Mona Lisa exactly where I want, I can specify a MARGIN. A margin is the amount of space between the element I want to position, Mona Lisa, and the edges of the element I want to position her on, the wall.

In this case, let's say that I want Mona Lisa to hang high, but not all the way at the top. I want a space, or MARGIN TOP, of 100px.

4 - Awesome, Mona Lisa isn't all the way at the ceiling anymore. But she's still all the way to the left of the wall. I don't want that because the door will obstruct the view once it swings open.

I'm going to specify a MARGIN LEFT of 150px so that we move her over to the right by that many pixels.

5 - Now she's exactly where I want her! But as I consider her awesomeness, I realize that I probably should have her framed. The BORDER attribute allows me to do just that - add a specific amount of pixels around the outer edge of my element.

Let's give Mona Lisa a nice white border of 20px.

6 - Now Mona Lisa is really standing out! Note that the total size of the element plus it's border has increased. It now includes the space taken up my Mona Lisa plus the space taken up by the frame. It's a total package!

That means that the margins we specified earlier are now calibrated to the edges of the frame around Mona Lisa, not just the actual painting itself.

7 - This may seem silly to you, but I have a lot of friends who aren't into art… or history… or pop culture… or common knowledge… so they don't really know what my painting is all about. In order to remedy that, I'll add some text within my element to make it very clear.

8 - The text begins at the very edge of the element, Mona Lisa. Because I have a white border and white writing, this makes it harder to read. In order to add space between the edge of my element, Mona Lisa, and the beginning of my text, I can specify PADDING.

Let's start off with a padding of 10px.

9 - Note that unlike specifying a margin, which adds transparent space between my two elements, the wall and the painting; a padding is not transparent. Adding a padding of 10px means that the size of my element has increased by 10px on all sides. Because my painting of Mona Lisa can't get any bigger, I have to glue a few Mona Lisa's together to accommodate the padding, which causes a repetition in the pattern.
