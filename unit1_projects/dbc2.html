A quick guide on how local businesses can find success on Facebook

Build your Facebook Page
Spread the Word
Keep the Conversation Going

Step 1: Build your Facebook Page

Go to facebook.com/page to open up a business page. Click on "Create Page" then follow each step in the guided turtorial.

Now that you have your page, change your profile photo to the logo for your business. Then choose a high-quality photo as your cover image. This can be a photo of your storefront or of the products you offer.

Be sure to seed your page with lots of contents. You want to make sure people have something to interact with once they find your page. Post alubums showcashing your products, special events, etc.

Step 2: Spread the word

Congratulations on finishing your Facebook Page! Now you'll want do as much promotion for your Page as possible. Start off by posting a link on your personal profile and inviting your friends and family to join. Ask your employees and business partners to do the same.

Be sure to include the your Facebook Page alongisde all other business info. Your website should link to your Page directly. Any stationary or business cards should include the full address. Consider add the "F" logo to your signage so that customers know where they can find you.

Do you have any partnerships with other businesses, or do you sponsor anyone locally? If so, ask these businesses or organizations to promote your Facebook page as well.

Step 3: Keep the Conversation Going

Now that you have a Page and customers connected to it, be sure to engage them. Post content on a regualr basis. A good rule of thumb is to start with at least a post a day. Tune up or down depending on the results and feedback you receive.

Stay "on brand" for all content that you post. That means making sure your business or products are always the center of attention. If your message is so generic it could have come from any Page "Happy Father's Day", consider revising it so that it feels more unique.

Use images that are visually appealing. Keep the text on the image to a minimum. Remember you're competing with cute babies and polished wedding photos - you'll want to bring your A game.

Step 4: Budget for Advertising

Set aside a marketing budget for Facebook. As little as $150 a month can make a big difference.

Go to facebook.com/ads/create and following the guide to create ads. Start off with one ad for the "Page Likes" objective, and another ad for the "Page Post Engagement" objective. This will ensure that you're spening money on both bringing people to your Page AND making sure they stay engaged.

When selecting the audience for your ads, take full advantage of the targeting capabilities. Keep it local to just a few miles from your business or zip code.

Step 5: Measure Your Results

When new customers call in, ask them where they heard about your business.

For online orders, consider using a Facebook code to keep track of where the orders are coming from.

In the posts you make on your Page, tell people to mention "Facebook" - this adds a sense of urgency and let's the people who see your content know they're special.
