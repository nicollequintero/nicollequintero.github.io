Reverse Engineering

June 29, 2014

Facebook

Problem: People are seperated from the people and things they care about the most, making staying connected difficult. Facebook aims to solve that by creating a virtual space for people and business to share what's going on in order to keep those who care fully informed.

Visual Challenges: So much info! So many ways to interact! The site is cluttered as a result. It's easy to lose track of what is native to the site, what content comes from friends or brands, and what is advertising.

Amazon

Problem: Finding an item you want (especially for the best price) is time-consuming and labor intensive, be it online or localled. Amazon aims to solve that by creating a robust online marketplace.

Visual Challenges: Making browsing efficient means displaying a large amount of item information - image, price, description, etc. all at once.

Netflix

Problem: Busy schedules means people want their entertainment on-demand. Instead of having to wait for strict airtime schedules, Netflix allows its members to browse as and when they place.

Visual Challenges: Can't think of any at the moment.
